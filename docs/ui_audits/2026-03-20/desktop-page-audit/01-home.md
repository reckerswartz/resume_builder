# Home

## Scope

- **Route**: `/`
- **Audience**: Logged-out visitors
- **Primary goal**: Move visitors into account creation or sign-in

## Strengths

- **Clear primary CTA**: The page makes the next move obvious with `Create account` and `Sign in` actions above the fold.
- **Compact first-screen layout**: The page fits comfortably into a full desktop viewport without forcing an immediate scroll.
- **Strong product framing**: The dark shell and white working panel already align well with the current Behance-derived direction.

## Findings

- **Medium - Technical copy is still too prominent**: Phrases like `Rails-first`, `Turbo powered`, `template switching`, and `tracked exports` explain implementation rather than user value. This makes the page feel more like a project demo than a polished product landing page.
- **Medium - The right-hand preview is decorative but not actionable**: The preview card helps visually, but it does not let visitors browse templates, inspect a live sample, or understand what makes the product better than a standard builder.
- **Low - CTA duplication creates mild competition**: The header and hero both promote account creation and sign-in. That is not broken, but it reduces the impact of the primary hero action area.
- **Medium - The page lacks trust and orientation content**: There is no short proof element, no sample outcomes, no explanation of who the product is for, and no quick way to understand the workflow beyond UI jargon.
- **Low - Decorative accents slightly over-accumulate**: The hero glow, dotted texture, dark preview block, metric cards, and product-cues card all draw attention on a page that only needs one strong message and one support panel.
- **Medium - Missing a secondary exploration path**: A visitor who is not ready to create an account has nowhere to go. A `Browse templates` or `See sample resume` path would reduce bounce risk.

## Recommended enhancements

- **Refocus the copy on outcomes**: Replace implementation-heavy messaging with benefits such as faster editing, easier revisions, clearer exports, and reduced context switching.
- **Make the support panel useful**: Turn the preview block into an interactive or linked product sample instead of a static decorative mockup.
- **Add lightweight trust content**: Include one short proof row, sample output callout, or concise workflow explainer beneath the hero.
